# Student Site Art Direction

## Overall Feel

- Keep the product student-facing, readable, and confidence-building.
- Use bolder blocks, clearer emphasis, and stronger hierarchy than a soft education SaaS layout.
- Avoid overly gentle, over-rounded, or uniformly polished card treatment.
- Keep the interface vivid and intentional, but not chaotic or experimental.

## Filter Hierarchy

- Treat Stage and Part as the primary filters.
- Treat Topics & Skills as a secondary narrowing layer.
- Keep Topics & Skills collapsed by default.
- Show only a limited set of high-value topics first, not the full long tail.
- Visible topics must always come from the current Stage + Part subset.
- If a selected topic becomes invalid after Stage or Part changes, remove it from UI state, filtering state, and URL state.

## Featured Interactive Card

- Keep it in the same family as the main site.
- Make it feel more energetic and more deliberate than standard document cards.
- Keep the title area and CTA more dominant than the supporting metadata.
- Avoid making every small label or marker use the same soft pill treatment.

## Support Panels

- Strategy, help, and trap sections should feel compact, high-value, and actionable.
- They should read more like exam guidance than explanatory notes.
- Prefer progressive disclosure for lower-priority guidance instead of showing everything at once.
