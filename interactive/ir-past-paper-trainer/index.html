    9701 IR routine and traps Open the compact exam-first checklist.
    4-step exam routine

    Start the paper the same way every time

    1. 1. Anchor the carbonyl. Check for C=O near 1700 cm⁻¹ before anything else.
    2. 2. Read the broad high region. Compare signals above 3000 cm⁻¹: alcohol O-H, acid O-H, or sharper N-H.
    3. 3. Use absence decisively. No broad O-H matters. No carbonyl matters.
    4. 4. Leave fingerprint for support. Use it to confirm the answer, not to start the answer.
    High-risk traps

    Kill these before they cost marks

    • Acid O-H is not normal alcohol O-H. Acid O-H is rougher and pushes into 2500–3000 cm⁻¹, usually with C=O.
    • Missing peaks are evidence. If the sharp ~1700 cm⁻¹ band is absent, rule carbonyl classes out early.
    Show one more trap

    Do not start with the fingerprint region. In 9701 it usually supports the answer after the diagnostic peaks are already fixed.
