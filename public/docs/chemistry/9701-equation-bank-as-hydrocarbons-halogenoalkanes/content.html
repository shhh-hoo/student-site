AS organic equations for hydrocarbons and halogenoalkanes.

AS organic chemistry

14 Hydrocarbons

Hydrogenation of alkenes to alkanes — H2, Pt/Ni, heat

  • Example. \( \ce{CH2=CH2(g) + H2(g) -> CH3CH3(g)} \)

Cracking of long-chain alkanes — heat, Al2O3

  • Example 1. \( \ce{C10H22 -> C8H18 + C2H4} \)
  • Example 2. \( \ce{C12H26 -> C6H14 + C6H12} \)

Note: Any balanced cracking example that gives smaller alkane / alkene products is acceptable.

Complete combustion of alkanes

  • Generic. \( \ce{CnH2n+2 + (3n+1)/2 O2 -> nCO2 + (n+1)H2O} \)
  • Methane. \( \ce{CH4(g) + 2O2(g) -> CO2(g) + 2H2O(l)} \)

Incomplete combustion of alkanes

  • To CO. \( \ce{2CH4(g) + 3O2(g) -> 2CO(g) + 4H2O(l)} \)
  • To C. \( \ce{CH4(g) + O2(g) -> C(s) + 2H2O(l)} \)

Free-radical substitution of ethane — Cl2 or Br2, UV light

  • Overall. \( \ce{C2H6 + Cl2 -> C2H5Cl + HCl} \)
  • Initiation. \( \ce{Cl2 -> 2Cl.} \)
  • Propagation 1. \( \ce{Cl. + C2H6 -> HCl + C2H5.} \)
  • Propagation 2. \( \ce{C2H5. + Cl2 -> C2H5Cl + Cl.} \)
  • Termination 1. \( \ce{Cl. + Cl. -> Cl2} \)
  • Termination 2. \( \ce{C2H5. + Cl. -> C2H5Cl} \)
  • Termination 3. \( \ce{C2H5. + C2H5. -> C4H10} \)

Elimination to form alkenes — ethanolic NaOH, heat

  • Example. \( \ce{CH3CH2Br(l) + NaOH(ethanol) -> CH2=CH2(g) + NaBr + H2O} \)

Dehydration of alcohols — heated Al2O3 or concentrated acid

  • Example. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH(l) -> CH2=CH2(g) + H2O(g)} \)

Electrophilic addition to alkenes: hydrogen

  • Example. \( \ce{CH2=CH2(g) + H2(g) -> CH3CH3(g)} \)

Electrophilic addition to alkenes: steam — H2O(g), H3PO4 catalyst

  • Example. \( \ce{CH2=CH2(g) + H2O(g) -> CH3CH2OH(l)} \)

Electrophilic addition to alkenes: HX

  • Ethene + HBr. \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + HBr -> CH3CH2Br} \)
  • Propene + HBr (Markovnikov major). \( \ce{CH3CH=CH2 + HBr -> CH3CHBrCH3} \)

Electrophilic addition to alkenes: halogen

  • Ethene + bromine. \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + Br2 -> BrCH2CH2Br} \)

Cold dilute acidified KMnO4 oxidation

  • Generic. \( \ce{RCH=CHR' + [O] + H2O -> RCH(OH)CH(OH)R'} \)
  • Ethene example. \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + [O] + H2O -> HOCH2CH2OH} \)

Hot concentrated acidified KMnO4 cleavage

  • Terminal alkene pattern. \( \ce{RCH=CH2 + 5[O] -> RCO2H + CO2 + H2O} \)
  • Internal alkene pattern. \( \ce{RCH=CHR' + 4[O] -> RCO2H + R'CO2H} \)
  • Propene example. \( \ce{CH3CH=CH2 + 5[O] -> CH3CO2H + CO2 + H2O} \)

Note: If a double-bond carbon has no H, that fragment gives a ketone rather than a carboxylic acid.

Addition polymerisation

  • Poly(ethene). \( \ce{nCH2=CH2 -> [-CH2-CH2-]n} \)
  • Poly(propene). \( \ce{nCH2=CHCH3 -> [-CH2-CH(CH3)-]n} \)
  • Poly(chloroethene) / PVC. \( \ce{nCH2=CHCl -> [-CH2-CHCl-]n} \)

15 Halogenoalkanes

Preparation from alkanes / alkenes / alcohols

  • Free-radical substitution. \( \ce{C2H6 + Cl2 -> C2H5Cl + HCl} \)
  • From alkene + HX. \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + HBr -> CH3CH2Br} \)
  • From alkene + Br2 (gives a dihalogenoalkane). \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + Br2 -> BrCH2CH2Br} \)
  • Alcohol + HBr. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + HBr -> CH3CH2Br + H2O} \)
  • Alcohol + HCl. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + HCl -> CH3CH2Cl + H2O} \)
  • Alcohol + PCl3. \( \ce{3CH3CH2OH + PCl3 -> 3CH3CH2Cl + H3PO3} \)
  • Alcohol + PCl5. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + PCl5 -> CH3CH2Cl + POCl3 + HCl} \)
  • Alcohol + SOCl2. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + SOCl2 -> CH3CH2Cl + SO2 + HCl} \)

Note: KCl + concentrated H2SO4 / H3PO4 is just an in situ way of generating HCl.

Nucleophilic substitution: aqueous hydroxide

  • Molecular. \( \ce{C2H5Br(l) + NaOH(aq) -> C2H5OH(l) + NaBr(aq)} \)
  • Net ionic. \( \ce{C2H5Br + OH- -> C2H5OH + Br-} \)

Nucleophilic substitution: cyanide

  • Molecular. \( \ce{C2H5Br + KCN(ethanol) -> C2H5CN + KBr} \)
  • Net ionic. \( \ce{C2H5Br + CN- -> C2H5CN + Br-} \)

Nucleophilic substitution: ammonia — ethanolic NH3, heated under pressure

  • Overall. \( \ce{C2H5Br + 2NH3 -> C2H5NH2 + NH4Br} \)
  • Net ionic style. \( \ce{C2H5Br + 2NH3 -> C2H5NH2 + NH4+ + Br-} \)

Test with aqueous AgNO3 in ethanol

  • Molecular. \( \ce{C2H5Br + AgNO3(aq) + H2O(l) -> C2H5OH(l) + AgBr(s) + HNO3(aq)} \)
  • Ionic emphasis. \( \ce{C2H5Br + Ag+ + H2O -> C2H5OH + AgBr + H+} \)

Elimination with ethanolic hydroxide

  • Molecular. \( \ce{C2H5Br + NaOH(ethanol) -> CH2=CH2 + NaBr + H2O} \)
  • Net ionic. \( \ce{C2H5Br + OH- -> CH2=CH2 + Br- + H2O} \)

SN2 one-step pattern

  • Example. \( \ce{CH3CH2Br + OH- -> CH3CH2OH + Br-} \)

SN1 two-step pattern

  • Step 1. \( \ce{(CH3)3CBr -> (CH3)3C+ + Br-} \)
  • Step 2. \( \ce{(CH3)3C+ + OH- -> (CH3)3COH} \)
