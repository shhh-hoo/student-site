AS foundations and inorganic reactions, grouped for quick retrieval.

AS Level equation bank

This section covers the AS content base. A Level students still need all of it.

AS foundational equations that are still routinely written

Ionisation energy (generic first IE)

  • Equation. \( \ce{X(g) -> X+(g) + e-} \)

Note: Include (g) throughout.

Successive ionisation energy (generic nth IE)

  • Equation. \( \ce{X(n-1)+(g) -> Xn+(g) + e-} \)

Ammonia as a Brønsted–Lowry base

  • Equation. \( \ce{NH3(aq) + H2O(l) <=> NH4+(aq) + OH-(aq)} \)

Formation of ammonium ion

  • Ionic. \( \ce{NH3 + H+ -> NH4+} \)
  • Molecular example. \( \ce{NH3(g) + HCl(g) -> NH4Cl(s)} \)

Neutralisation

  • Net ionic. \( \ce{H+(aq) + OH-(aq) -> H2O(l)} \)

AS inorganic chemistry

9 Period 3

Elements with oxygen — core products named in the syllabus

  • Sodium. \( \ce{4Na(s) + O2(g) -> 2Na2O(s)} \)
  • Magnesium. \( \ce{2Mg(s) + O2(g) -> 2MgO(s)} \)
  • Aluminium. \( \ce{4Al(s) + 3O2(g) -> 2Al2O3(s)} \)
  • Phosphorus. \( \ce{P4(s) + 5O2(g) -> P4O10(s)} \)
  • Sulfur. \( \ce{S(s) + O2(g) -> SO2(g)} \)

Note: Na2O2 may be seen in some teaching notes, but the syllabus-named oxide product is Na2O.

Elements with chlorine

  • Sodium. \( \ce{2Na(s) + Cl2(g) -> 2NaCl(s)} \)
  • Magnesium. \( \ce{Mg(s) + Cl2(g) -> MgCl2(s)} \)
  • Aluminium. \( \ce{2Al(s) + 3Cl2(g) -> 2AlCl3(s)} \)
  • Silicon. \( \ce{Si(s) + 2Cl2(g) -> SiCl4(l)} \)
  • Phosphorus. \( \ce{P4(s) + 10Cl2(g) -> 4PCl5(s)} \)

Elements with water — Na and Mg only are specified in 9.2.1

  • Sodium. \( \ce{2Na(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2NaOH(aq) + H2(g)} \)
  • Magnesium with cold water. \( \ce{Mg(s) + 2H2O(l) -> Mg(OH)2(s) + H2(g)} \)
  • Magnesium with steam. \( \ce{Mg(s) + H2O(g) -> MgO(s) + H2(g)} \)

Oxides with water

  • Sodium oxide. \( \ce{Na2O(s) + H2O(l) -> 2NaOH(aq)} \)
  • Magnesium oxide. \( \ce{MgO(s) + H2O(l) -> Mg(OH)2(s)} \)
  • Aluminium oxide. No reaction with water
  • Silicon(IV) oxide. No reaction with water
  • Phosphorus(V) oxide. \( \ce{P4O10(s) + 6H2O(l) -> 4H3PO4(aq)} \)
  • Sulfur dioxide. \( \ce{SO2(g) + H2O(l) <=> H2SO3(aq)} \)
  • Sulfur trioxide. \( \ce{SO3(g) + H2O(l) -> H2SO4(aq)} \)

Basic / amphoteric / acidic oxide equations

  • Na2O with acid. \( \ce{Na2O(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> 2NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • Na2O net ionic. \( \ce{O2- + 2H+ -> H2O} \)
  • MgO with acid. \( \ce{MgO(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> MgCl2(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • MgO net ionic. \( \ce{MgO(s) + 2H+(aq) -> Mg2+(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • Al2O3 with acid. \( \ce{Al2O3(s) + 6HCl(aq) -> 2AlCl3(aq) + 3H2O(l)} \)
  • Al2O3 with alkali. \( \ce{Al2O3(s) + 2NaOH(aq) + 3H2O(l) -> 2Na[Al(OH)4](aq)} \)
  • Al2O3 ionic alkali form. \( \ce{Al2O3(s) + 2OH-(aq) + 3H2O(l) -> 2[Al(OH)4]-(aq)} \)
  • SiO2 with alkali. \( \ce{SiO2(s) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Na2SiO3(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • P4O10 with alkali. \( \ce{P4O10(s) + 12NaOH(aq) -> 4Na3PO4(aq) + 6H2O(l)} \)
  • SO2 with alkali. \( \ce{SO2(g) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Na2SO3(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • SO2 excess gas. \( \ce{Na2SO3(aq) + SO2(g) + H2O(l) -> 2NaHSO3(aq)} \)
  • SO3 with alkali. \( \ce{SO3(g) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Na2SO4(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • SO3 excess gas. \( \ce{Na2SO4(aq) + SO3(g) + H2O(l) -> 2NaHSO4(aq)} \)

Hydroxides in acid / alkali

  • NaOH with acid. \( \ce{NaOH(aq) + HCl(aq) -> NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • Net ionic. \( \ce{H+(aq) + OH-(aq) -> H2O(l)} \)
  • Mg(OH)2 with acid. \( \ce{Mg(OH)2(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> MgCl2(aq) + 2H2O(l)} \)
  • Al(OH)3 with acid. \( \ce{Al(OH)3(s) + 3HCl(aq) -> AlCl3(aq) + 3H2O(l)} \)
  • Al(OH)3 with alkali. \( \ce{Al(OH)3(s) + NaOH(aq) -> Na[Al(OH)4](aq)} \)

Note: Older teaching notes sometimes write sodium aluminate; the aqueous tetrahydroxoaluminate form is the clearer modern version.

Period 3 chlorides with water

  • NaCl. \( \ce{NaCl(s) -> Na+(aq) + Cl-(aq)} \)
  • MgCl2 hydration / acidity. \( \ce{MgCl2(s) + 6H2O(l) -> [Mg(H2O)6]2+(aq) + 2Cl-(aq)} \)
  • Mg aqua-ion hydrolysis. \( \ce{[Mg(H2O)6]2+(aq) + H2O(l) <=> [Mg(H2O)5(OH)]+(aq) + H3O+(aq)} \)
  • AlCl3 hydration. \( \ce{AlCl3(s) + 6H2O(l) -> [Al(H2O)6]3+(aq) + 3Cl-(aq)} \)
  • Al aqua-ion hydrolysis. \( \ce{[Al(H2O)6]3+(aq) + H2O(l) <=> [Al(H2O)5(OH)]2+(aq) + H3O+(aq)} \)
  • SiCl4 hydrolysis. \( \ce{SiCl4(l) + 2H2O(l) -> SiO2(s) + 4HCl(g)} \)
  • PCl3 hydrolysis. \( \ce{PCl3(l) + 3H2O(l) -> H3PO3(aq) + 3HCl(aq)} \)
  • PCl5 step 1. \( \ce{PCl5(s) + H2O(l) -> POCl3(l) + 2HCl(g)} \)
  • POCl3 hydrolysis. \( \ce{POCl3(l) + 3H2O(l) -> H3PO4(aq) + 3HCl(aq)} \)
  • PCl5 overall. \( \ce{PCl5(s) + 4H2O(l) -> H3PO4(aq) + 5HCl(aq)} \)

Period 3 chlorides with aqueous alkali

  • MgCl2. \( \ce{MgCl2(aq) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Mg(OH)2(s) + 2NaCl(aq)} \)
  • AlCl3. \( \ce{AlCl3(aq) + 3NaOH(aq) -> Al(OH)3(s) + 3NaCl(aq)} \)
  • SiCl4. \( \ce{SiCl4(l) + 4NaOH(aq) -> SiO2(s) + 4NaCl(aq) + 2H2O(l)} \)
  • PCl5. \( \ce{PCl5(s) + 8NaOH(aq) -> Na3PO4(aq) + 5NaCl(aq) + 4H2O(l)} \)

10 Group 2 (magnesium to barium)

Metals with oxygen

  • Generic. \( \ce{2M(s) + O2(g) -> 2MO(s)} \)
  • Examples. \( \ce{2Mg(s) + O2(g) -> 2MgO(s)} \); \( \ce{2Ca(s) + O2(g) -> 2CaO(s)} \)

Metals with water

  • Magnesium, cold water. \( \ce{Mg(s) + 2H2O(l) -> Mg(OH)2(s) + H2(g)} \)
  • Magnesium, steam. \( \ce{Mg(s) + H2O(g) -> MgO(s) + H2(g)} \)
  • Calcium. \( \ce{Ca(s) + 2H2O(l) -> Ca(OH)2(aq) + H2(g)} \)
  • Strontium. \( \ce{Sr(s) + 2H2O(l) -> Sr(OH)2(aq) + H2(g)} \)
  • Barium. \( \ce{Ba(s) + 2H2O(l) -> Ba(OH)2(aq) + H2(g)} \)

Metals with dilute acids

  • With HCl (generic). \( \ce{M(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> MCl2(aq) + H2(g)} \)
  • Net ionic. \( \ce{M(s) + 2H+(aq) -> M2+(aq) + H2(g)} \)
  • With H2SO4 (generic). \( \ce{M(s) + H2SO4(aq) -> MSO4(aq/s) + H2(g)} \)

Note: Down the group, the sulfate may become less soluble and slow the reaction.

Oxides with water

  • Generic. \( \ce{MO(s) + H2O(l) -> M(OH)2(aq/s)} \)
  • Examples. \( \ce{MgO + H2O -> Mg(OH)2} \); \( \ce{CaO + H2O -> Ca(OH)2} \); \( \ce{SrO + H2O -> Sr(OH)2} \); \( \ce{BaO + H2O -> Ba(OH)2} \)

Oxides with acids

  • With HCl. \( \ce{MO(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> MCl2(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • With H2SO4. \( \ce{MO(s) + H2SO4(aq) -> MSO4(aq/s) + H2O(l)} \)

Hydroxides with acids

  • With HCl. \( \ce{M(OH)2 + 2HCl(aq) -> MCl2(aq) + 2H2O(l)} \)
  • With H2SO4. \( \ce{M(OH)2 + H2SO4(aq) -> MSO4(aq/s) + 2H2O(l)} \)

Carbonates with acids

  • With HCl. \( \ce{MCO3(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> MCl2(aq) + H2O(l) + CO2(g)} \)
  • With H2SO4. \( \ce{MCO3(s) + H2SO4(aq) -> MSO4(aq/s) + H2O(l) + CO2(g)} \)
  • Net ionic. \( \ce{CO3^2-(s/aq) + 2H+(aq) -> CO2(g) + H2O(l)} \)

Thermal decomposition

  • Carbonates. \( \ce{MCO3(s) -> MO(s) + CO2(g)} \)
  • Nitrates. \( \ce{2M(NO3)2(s) -> 2MO(s) + 4NO2(g) + O2(g)} \)

11 Group 17

Halogens with hydrogen

  • Chlorine. \( \ce{H2(g) + Cl2(g) -> 2HCl(g)} \)
  • Bromine. \( \ce{H2(g) + Br2(g) -> 2HBr(g)} \)
  • Iodine. \( \ce{H2(g) + I2(s) <=> 2HI(g)} \)

Halogen displacement (ionic forms)

  • Chlorine + bromide. \( \ce{Cl2(aq) + 2Br-(aq) -> 2Cl-(aq) + Br2(aq)} \)
  • Chlorine + iodide. \( \ce{Cl2(aq) + 2I-(aq) -> 2Cl-(aq) + I2(aq/s)} \)
  • Bromine + iodide. \( \ce{Br2(aq) + 2I-(aq) -> 2Br-(aq) + I2(aq/s)} \)

Silver nitrate tests for halides

  • Chloride. \( \ce{Ag+(aq) + Cl-(aq) -> AgCl(s)} \)
  • Bromide. \( \ce{Ag+(aq) + Br-(aq) -> AgBr(s)} \)
  • Iodide. \( \ce{Ag+(aq) + I-(aq) -> AgI(s)} \)
  • AgCl dissolves in NH3. \( \ce{AgCl(s) + 2NH3(aq) -> [Ag(NH3)2]+(aq) + Cl-(aq)} \)
  • AgBr dissolves in concentrated NH3. \( \ce{AgBr(s) + 2NH3(aq) -> [Ag(NH3)2]+(aq) + Br-(aq)} \)

Note: AgI is insoluble in ammonia.

Halides with concentrated sulfuric acid

  • Fluoride. \( \ce{F-(s/aq) + H2SO4(l) -> HF(g) + HSO4-(aq)} \)
  • Chloride. \( \ce{Cl-(s/aq) + H2SO4(l) -> HCl(g) + HSO4-(aq)} \)
  • Bromide overall. \( \ce{2Br-(s/aq) + 2H+(aq) + H2SO4(l) -> Br2 + SO2 + 2H2O(l)} \)
  • Bromide oxidation half-equation. \( \ce{2Br- -> Br2 + 2e-} \)
  • Sulfuric acid reduction half-equation. \( \ce{H2SO4 + 2H+ + 2e- -> SO2 + 2H2O} \)
  • Iodide overall. \( \ce{8I-(s/aq) + 8H+(aq) + H2SO4(l) -> 4I2 + H2S + 4H2O(l)} \)
  • Iodide oxidation half-equation. \( \ce{2I- -> I2 + 2e-} \)
  • Sulfuric acid reduction to H2S. \( \ce{H2SO4 + 8H+ + 8e- -> H2S + 4H2O} \)

Chlorine with aqueous sodium hydroxide

  • Cold, dilute molecular. \( \ce{Cl2(aq) + 2NaOH(aq) -> NaCl(aq) + NaClO(aq) + H2O(l)} \)
  • Cold, dilute ionic. \( \ce{Cl2 + 2OH- -> Cl- + ClO- + H2O} \)
  • Hot, concentrated molecular. \( \ce{3Cl2(aq) + 6NaOH(aq) -> 5NaCl(aq) + NaClO3(aq) + 3H2O(l)} \)
  • Hot, concentrated ionic. \( \ce{3Cl2 + 6OH- -> 5Cl- + ClO3- + 3H2O} \)

Chlorine in water purification

  • Hydrolysis. \( \ce{Cl2(aq) + H2O(l) <=> HCl(aq) + HOCl(aq)} \)
  • Weak acid dissociation. \( \ce{HOCl(aq) <=> H+(aq) + ClO-(aq)} \)

12 Nitrogen and sulfur

Ammonia and ammonium

  • Base behavior. \( \ce{NH3(aq) + H2O(l) <=> NH4+(aq) + OH-(aq)} \)
  • Ammonium formation. \( \ce{NH3 + H+ -> NH4+} \)
  • Ammonium salt + alkali. \( \ce{NH4+(aq) + OH-(aq) -> NH3(g) + H2O(l)} \)

Oxides of nitrogen in engines / atmosphere

  • Formation of NO. \( \ce{N2(g) + O2(g) -> 2NO(g)} \)
  • Oxidation to NO2. \( \ce{2NO(g) + O2(g) -> 2NO2(g)} \)
  • Catalytic removal by CO. \( \ce{2NO(g) + 2CO(g) -> N2(g) + 2CO2(g)} \)

Note: The balanced car-engine removal equation is 2NO + 2CO -> N2 + 2CO2.

Acid rain pathway involving NOx and SO2

  • Catalytic oxidation step. \( \ce{SO2(g) + NO2(g) -> SO3(g) + NO(g)} \)
  • Acid formation. \( \ce{SO3(g) + H2O(l) -> H2SO4(aq)} \)
