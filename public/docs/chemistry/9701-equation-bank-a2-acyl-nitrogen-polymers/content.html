A2 equations for acyl chemistry, nitrogen compounds, amino acids, and condensation polymers.

33 Carboxylic acids and derivatives

Benzoic acid preparation

  • Methylbenzene oxidation. \( \ce{C6H5CH3 + 3[O] -> C6H5CO2H + H2O} \)

Carboxylic acid -> acyl chloride

  • With PCl3. \( \ce{3RCO2H + PCl3 -> 3RCOCl + H3PO3} \)
  • With PCl5. \( \ce{RCO2H + PCl5 -> RCOCl + POCl3 + HCl} \)
  • With SOCl2. \( \ce{RCO2H + SOCl2 -> RCOCl + SO2 + HCl} \)

Further oxidation of named acids

  • Methanoic acid. \( \ce{HCOOH + [O] -> CO2 + H2O} \)
  • Ethanedioic acid. \( \ce{HOOCCOOH + [O] -> 2CO2 + H2O} \)

Esters from acyl chlorides

  • With alcohol. \( \ce{RCOCl + R'OH -> RCOOR' + HCl} \)
  • With phenol. \( \ce{RCOCl + ArOH -> RCOOAr + HCl} \)

Acyl chloride reactions

  • Hydrolysis. \( \ce{RCOCl + H2O -> RCOOH + HCl} \)
  • With ammonia, stepwise. \( \ce{RCOCl + NH3 -> RCONH2 + HCl} \)
  • With ammonia, overall. \( \ce{RCOCl + 2NH3 -> RCONH2 + NH4Cl} \)
  • With primary amine, stepwise. \( \ce{RCOCl + R'NH2 -> RCONHR' + HCl} \)
  • With primary amine, overall. \( \ce{RCOCl + 2R'NH2 -> RCONHR' + R'NH3Cl} \)

34 Nitrogen compounds

Primary and secondary amines

  • Primary amine from halogenoalkane. \( \ce{RBr + 2NH3 -> RNH2 + NH4Br} \)
  • Secondary amine from halogenoalkane + primary amine. \( \ce{RBr + 2R'NH2 -> RR'NH + R'NH3Br} \)
  • Reduction of nitrile. \( \ce{RCN + 4[H] -> RCH2NH2} \)
  • Reduction of amide. \( \ce{RCONH2 + 4[H] -> RCH2NH2 + H2O} \)

Basicity of amines

  • Generic. \( \ce{RNH2 + H2O <=> RNH3+ + OH-} \)
  • Ammonia. \( \ce{NH3 + H2O <=> NH4+ + OH-} \)

Phenylamine preparation and reactions

  • Benzene nitration. \( \ce{C6H6 + HNO3 -> C6H5NO2 + H2O} \)
  • Nitrobenzene reduction. \( \ce{C6H5NO2 + 6[H] -> C6H5NH2 + 2H2O} \)
  • Phenylamine + bromine water. \( \ce{C6H5NH2 + 3Br2(aq) -> C6H2Br3NH2(s) + 3HBr} \)
  • Diazotisation. \( \ce{C6H5NH2 + NaNO2 + 2HCl -> C6H5N2+Cl- + NaCl + 2H2O} \)
  • Diazonium hydrolysis to phenol. \( \ce{C6H5N2+Cl- + H2O -> C6H5OH + N2 + HCl} \)
  • Azo coupling with phenol. \( \ce{C6H5N2+Cl- + C6H5OH + NaOH -> HOC6H4N=NC6H5 + NaCl + H2O} \)

Amides

  • From ammonia + acyl chloride. \( \ce{RCOCl + 2NH3 -> RCONH2 + NH4Cl} \)
  • From primary amine + acyl chloride. \( \ce{RCOCl + 2R'NH2 -> RCONHR' + R'NH3Cl} \)
  • Acid hydrolysis. \( \ce{RCONH2 + H2O + H+ -> RCOOH + NH4+} \)
  • Alkaline hydrolysis. \( \ce{RCONH2 + OH- -> RCOO- + NH3} \)
  • Reduction. \( \ce{RCONH2 + 4[H] -> RCH2NH2 + H2O} \)

Amino acids and peptides

  • Zwitterion form. \( \ce{H2NCH(R)CO2H <=> +H3NCH(R)CO2-} \)
  • In acid. \( \ce{H2NCH(R)CO2H + H+ -> +H3NCH(R)CO2H} \)
  • In alkali. \( \ce{H2NCH(R)CO2H + OH- -> H2NCH(R)CO2- + H2O} \)
  • Peptide formation. \( \ce{H2NCH(R)CO2H + H2NCH(R')CO2H -> H2NCH(R)CONHCH(R')CO2H + H2O} \)

35 Polymerisation

Polyesters

  • Diol + dicarboxylic acid. \( \ce{nHO-R-OH + nHOOC-R'-COOH -> [-O-R-OCO-R'-CO-]n + 2nH2O} \)
  • Diol + dioyl chloride. \( \ce{nHO-R-OH + nClOC-R'-COCl -> [-O-R-OCO-R'-CO-]n + 2nHCl} \)
  • Hydroxycarboxylic acid. \( \ce{nHO-R-COOH -> [-O-R-CO-]n + nH2O} \)

Polyamides

  • Diamine + dicarboxylic acid. \( \ce{nH2N-R-NH2 + nHOOC-R'-COOH -> [-NH-R-NHCO-R'-CO-]n + 2nH2O} \)
  • Diamine + dioyl chloride. \( \ce{nH2N-R-NH2 + nClOC-R'-COCl -> [-NH-R-NHCO-R'-CO-]n + 2nHCl} \)
  • Aminocarboxylic acid. \( \ce{nH2N-R-COOH -> [-NH-R-CO-]n + nH2O} \)
  • Amino acid. \( \ce{nH2NCH(R)COOH -> [-NHCH(R)CO-]n + nH2O} \)

Relative hydrolysis ease

Note: Acyl chlorides hydrolyse fastest because the C=O group and Cl both withdraw electron density.

Note: Halogenoalkanes hydrolyse more slowly; halogenoarenes are much less reactive because the C-Cl bond has partial double-bond character through delocalisation.
