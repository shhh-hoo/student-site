AS alcohol and carbonyl chemistry, including oxidation, reduction, tests, and HCN addition.

16 Alcohols

Preparation and interconversion of alcohols

  • From alkene + steam. \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + H2O -> CH3CH2OH} \)
  • From alkene oxidation (gives a diol). \( \ce{CH2=CH2 + [O] + H2O -> HOCH2CH2OH} \)
  • From halogenoalkane. \( \ce{C2H5Br + NaOH(aq) -> C2H5OH + NaBr} \)
  • From aldehyde reduction. \( \ce{CH3CHO + 2[H] -> CH3CH2OH} \)
  • From ketone reduction. \( \ce{CH3COCH3 + 2[H] -> CH3CH(OH)CH3} \)
  • From carboxylic acid reduction. \( \ce{CH3CO2H + 4[H] -> CH3CH2OH + H2O} \)
  • From ester acid hydrolysis. \( \ce{CH3CO2C2H5 + H2O <=> CH3CO2H + C2H5OH} \)
  • From ester alkaline hydrolysis. \( \ce{CH3CO2C2H5 + OH- -> CH3CO2- + C2H5OH} \)

Combustion of ethanol

  • Equation. \( \ce{C2H5OH(l) + 3O2(g) -> 2CO2(g) + 3H2O(l)} \)

Alcohol + sodium

  • Equation. \( \ce{2C2H5OH(l) + 2Na(s) -> 2C2H5ONa + H2(g)} \)

Alcohol -> halogenoalkane

  • With HBr. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + HBr -> CH3CH2Br + H2O} \)
  • With PCl5. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + PCl5 -> CH3CH2Cl + POCl3 + HCl} \)

Oxidation of primary and secondary alcohols

  • Primary, distil. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + [O] -> CH3CHO + H2O} \)
  • Primary, reflux. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + 2[O] -> CH3CO2H + H2O} \)
  • Secondary. \( \ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3 + [O] -> CH3COCH3 + H2O} \)
  • Tertiary. No oxidation under these syllabus conditions

Dehydration

  • Equation. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH -> CH2=CH2 + H2O} \)

Esterification — concentrated H2SO4 catalyst

  • Equation. \( \ce{CH3CO2H + C2H5OH <=> CH3CO2C2H5 + H2O} \)

Iodoform test for CH3CH(OH)-

  • Generic. \( \ce{CH3CH(OH)R + 4I2 + 6OH- -> RCOO- + CHI3(s) + 5I- + 5H2O} \)
  • Ethanol case. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + 4I2 + 6OH- -> HCOO- + CHI3(s) + 5I- + 5H2O} \)

17 Carbonyl compounds

Preparation

  • Aldehyde from primary alcohol. \( \ce{CH3CH2OH + [O] -> CH3CHO + H2O} \)
  • Ketone from secondary alcohol. \( \ce{CH3CH(OH)CH3 + [O] -> CH3COCH3 + H2O} \)

Reduction

  • Aldehyde. \( \ce{RCHO + 2[H] -> RCH2OH} \)
  • Ketone. \( \ce{RCOR' + 2[H] -> RCH(OH)R'} \)

Nucleophilic addition of HCN

  • Ethanal. \( \ce{CH3CHO + HCN <=> CH3CH(OH)CN} \)
  • Propanone. \( \ce{(CH3)2CO + HCN <=> (CH3)2C(OH)CN} \)

HCN mechanism support equations

  • Aldehyde, step 1. \( \ce{RCHO + CN- -> RCH(O-)CN} \)
  • Aldehyde, step 2. \( \ce{RCH(O-)CN + H+ -> RCH(OH)CN} \)
  • Ketone, step 1. \( \ce{RCOR' + CN- -> RC(O-)(CN)R'} \)
  • Ketone, step 2. \( \ce{RC(O-)(CN)R' + H+ -> RC(OH)(CN)R'} \)

2,4-DNPH test

  • Generic condensation. \( \ce{R2C=O + H2NNHC6H3(NO2)2 -> R2C=NNHC6H3(NO2)2 + H2O} \)

Note: The visible result is the orange precipitate.

Fehling's and Tollens' equations

  • Aldehyde net ionic with Fehling's. \( \ce{RCHO + 2Cu2+ + 5OH- -> RCOO- + Cu2O(s) + 3H2O} \)
  • Aldehyde net ionic with Tollens'. \( \ce{RCHO + 2[Ag(NH3)2]+ + 3OH- -> RCOO- + 2Ag(s) + 4NH3 + 2H2O} \)

Iodoform test for CH3CO-

  • Generic. \( \ce{RCOCH3 + 3I2 + 4OH- -> RCOO- + CHI3(s) + 3I- + 3H2O} \)
